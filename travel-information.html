Travel Information

NIT Warangal is located at a distance of 140 km from the capital city of Hyderabad on the National Highway 202.


Warangal by Air


Rajiv Gandhi International Airport in Hyderabad is the nearest airport and is 170 kilometers from Warangal city. There will be a Help Desk pitched at the airport from 9th Dec. 2015 to 12th Dec. 2015, with a banner stating “Welcome to the Delegates of 7th IEEE International Conference on Technology for Education (T4E 2015)” to assist you. A Taxi from NIT Warangal can be arranged on prior request. (Call 09866440460) (It will cost you about Rs.3500/-). Pre-paid taxis are available at the airport to bring you to NIT Warangal (this is expensive). Alternatively, there are Air Conditioned aero buses (named Pushpak) plying from Airport to Secunderabad Railway station (Charges Rs. 250/- per person) with a frequency of 1 hour. Regular Trains are available from Secunderabad Railway Station to Kazipet station. There will be assistance at Kazipet Railway station to guide you to NIT Warangal which is just 3 km away from the railway station.


Warangal by Railways


Nearest Railway Station: Kazipet Jn. NIT Warangal is 3 km away from Kazipet railway station and 12 km from Warangal railway station. Delegates are advised to alight at Kazipet railway station, if their train is routed through it or at Warangal Railway Station, if Kazipet Railway station is not in their route. A number of auto-rickshaws will be available at the railway stations to fetch you to NIT Warangal. There will be assistance booth at Kazipet Railway station to guide you to NIT Warangal. On prior information, Car pick up could be arranged from Kazipet Railway Station.


Warangal by Road


NIT Warangal is well connected by road from Hyderabad Central Bus Station (CBS). Regular A/c buses as well as Non A/c with sign board : “ Hanamkonda” or “Warangal” are available with a frequency of 10 minutes. All the buses from Hyderabad stop in front of the Main Gate of NIT Warangal on request. NIT Guest House is at a walkable distance from the Main Gate.


Tourist Attractions at Warangal:


Warangal, once the capital of the Kakatiya Kingdom, is the Second largest city of Telangana State. Warangal's History, with beautiful lakes, fine temples, rich fauna & flora have contributed to its importance as a Tourist Centre. The historical monuments of this region with excellent architectural styles have been attracting tourists fom various parts of the world. The twin cities of Warangal and Hanamkonda are well known tourist attractions with various site seeing places like Warangal Fort, Temple Hill of Bhadrakali, Thousand Pillar Temple, Ramappa Temple, the vast and dense forests of Eturnagaram and Pakhal, Lakes, Animal Sanctuaries, Zoological Park, Science Museums, Planetarium etc. For further queries please contact: 0988440460, 08332969704; Email : iakreddy@nitw.ac.in , iakreddy@yahoo.com
